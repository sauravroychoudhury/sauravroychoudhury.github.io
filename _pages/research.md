My research lies at the intersection of financial economics, artificial intelligence, public policy, and regional economic development. A growing focus of my work applies machine learning and large language models (LLMs) to problems in finance — such as insolvency and credit-risk prediction — alongside economic-impact and regional modeling of infrastructure and policy decisions.

## Research Interests

- Artificial Intelligence and Large Language Models (LLMs) in Finance
- Economic Impact Analysis and Regional Economic Modeling (IMPLAN, REMI, and RIMS II)
- Financial Economics and Corporate Governance
- Public Policy Evaluation and Economic Freedom
- Leveraged ETFs, Volatility, and Financial Market Dynamics
- Innovation, Regulation, and Entrepreneurship

For a detailed list of published articles, book chapters, policy reports, and working papers, see my [Publications](/publications/) page.
